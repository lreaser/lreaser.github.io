  • NSDA Information

    The National Speech and Debate Association is more than just a debate circuit. It is an honor society for speech and debate competitors. All students are strongly encouraged to join the NSDA for a nominal fee ($20) after their second tournament. You gain several perks for doing so.

    First, students can attend the NSDA state qualifier, an excellent local tournament. You may also advance to nationals, which occurs during the last week of school.

    Second, students begin to log points with the NSDA. Points are awarded on the basis of performance at congress tournaments and other speech/debate events.

    Students may also receive points for doing speaking or service outside of competitions. Giving a speech at a school assembly for example, earns points. Any speaking activity in front of 15 or more people qualifies for points.

    Points may be retroactively entered for a school year upon joining. To log points not pertaining to Congress, such as speech/MUN/drama/service events, please click here:NSDA Service Points Entry Points earned at Congress tournament will automatically be logged by Mrs. Bakke.

    If you do not have NSDA account and would like to register for one, please sign up here: NSDA Registration Form These points have numerous advantages. Upon earning a certain number of points, different levels of certification are awarded. The highest level that can be earned is "Premier Distinction" at 1500 points. Colleges are familiar with the NSDA and levels of distinction.

    Certain prestigious awards can also be obtained. If a student has over 750 points, a student may receive the "Academic All American Award" upon recommendation by the coach for exemplary leadership.

    For those interested in learning more about the NSDA, further NSDA information can be found here:NSDA Honor Society For those who wish to watch past NSDA Rounds Click here: National Round Videos
