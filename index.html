What is Congress?

TJ Congressional Debate

Eloquence

The importance of delivery differentiates Congress from other types of debate. Congress is not just a listing of facts, but also an attempt to educate and persuade, to appeal to one's humanity through meaningful rhetoric. Your words and your arguments are powerful, but only when you know how to communicate them.

Research

Unlike other debate events, you will have a multitude of foreign, domestic, and economic topics to debate at every tournament. Learn how to research recent news events and analyze articles correctly, incorporating evidence such as statistics, quotes, and narratives into your arguments.

Argumentation

Your content analysis is what gives substance to your speaking. Learn how to set framework, structure constructive and opposition points, flow rounds, and fluently craft summation speeches. We teach you not only to form written arguments before tournaments like in a traditional debate setting, but also how to think analytically on the fly and craft witty arguments and cross examination rebuttals extemporaneously.

Sociopolitical Awareness

What makes Congress the most unique is the importance of humanity. Through your research, you will quickly recognize the inherent inequalities that exist not only in our country, but across the globe. In effort to persuade, competitors will often draw upon these humanitarian issues in round. In the process of research and debate, you will learn about the complex political world around us and empathize with those whose voices aren't heard.
